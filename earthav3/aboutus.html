Who We Are

Eartha is an independent organisation providing consultancy, training, and research & evaluation services to private, public and voluntary organisations in the employability and education sectors.
Our expertise is two-fold:
  1. the design of employability and education services for a variety of learners;
  2. the people and organisational strategy that underlies the effective delivery of those services.
Founded by an Occupational Psychologist, our aim is to support your business with evidence-based and tailored support based on psychological theory.
Occupational Psychology is at the heart of explaining and predicting behaviour in the workplace enabling us to provide practical and applied insights that can improve wellbeing, performance and society.

Our Aim and Values

Our values inform how we work with our clients and each other. eartha aims to be ethical, flexible and useful, underpinned by four main values:

Personalisation – working closely and openly with clients, each project is designed and delivered to meet their specific needs.
Practicable – recommendations and solutions should be realistic and able to be applied in practice.
Proportionate – we conduct our business in an ethical manner, and aim to deliver an appropriate and cost-effective service.
Positive – we want to make a positive impact on our clients and society.

Director/Founder

Dr Joanna Butler CPsychol AFBPsS MSc

A Chartered Occupational Psychologist with over 15 years’ experience in the education and employability sectors, her expertise lies in evidence-based personal and organisational development. After more than 12 years as an integral part of senior management teams, Joanna has led multiple successful projects within the context of fast-paced and dynamic international welfare organisations, providing research and consultancy services to clients delivering employability and skills programmes across the UK, Europe and the Middle East. She also lectures at the University of Strathclyde Business School on Human Resource Management and Work Psychology. During her career, Joanna has collaborated with individuals from diverse social and cultural backgrounds, having worked with university students, HR practitioners, chief executives, government officials, and unemployed jobseekers. Joanna obtained an MSc and PhD from the University of Strathclyde and is qualified in Psychometric Testing (Level A and B). She is also an Associate Fellow of the British Psychological Society and registered Practitioner Psychologist with the Health and Care Professions Council.
