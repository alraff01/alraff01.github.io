What We Do

We support organisations delivering employability and education services with consultancy, research & evaluation, and training to make evidence-based strategy decisions, enable staff development and improve performance.

  • Learn More
eartha has primarily worked within the public sector across government organisations, welfare and employability providers, and universities. Specifically, we work across four main areas:
  • research and evaluation
  • consultancy and strategy
  • training and education
  • 1:1 coaching

Service Offer

The application of psychological theory to your business needs is at the forefront of eartha’s service offer, which includes:
  • Design and evaluation of employability and welfare provision
  • Delivery of bespoke training, e.g. staff development programmes
  • Organisational analysis and change strategies
  • Advice and guidance for Human Resource professionals
  • Monitoring and evaluation (and data analysis) strategy
  • Support successful bid writing
  • Research to develop an organisation’s knowledge bank and guide operational delivery
  • Coaching to achieve professional or academic goals.

Some achievements include:

  • Strategic service design of employability support for long-term unemployed jobseekers, resulting in top quartile national league table performance.
  • Design / delivery of an organisational change strategy which improved staff stress and job satisfaction, while also increased organisational performance by 30% (minimum).
  • Established a skills department to deliver employability and soft skills training to 20,000+ learners, contributing a min of 20% (above average) to organisational outcomes.
  • Advised the Saudi government on the realities and pitfalls of delivering vocational education to socially excluded groups, and collaborated to develop a skills organisation.
  • Delivered training to 2000+ professionals across 25+ courses on subjects in Organisational Behaviour, Work Psychology, Human Resource Management and Employee Reward.
  • Integral member of bid team successfully securing the largest Government procured employability programme in UK, the Work Programme, by designing services for disadvantaged jobseekers.
  • Coached senior management and professionals to promotion; poor performers to exceed targets; and students to obtain first class results.
